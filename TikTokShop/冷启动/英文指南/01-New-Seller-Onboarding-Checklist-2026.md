# TikTok Shop New Seller Onboarding Checklist 2026: The Compliance-First Setup Guide

## Your AHR Starts at 200. The Clock Starts at Order #1.

A new TikTok Shop seller shipped 14 orders in week one. Three were late. That's a 21.4% LDR on day seven, and TikTok was already watching.

Your Account Health Rating starts at 200 points the moment your shop goes live, and the same thresholds that apply to a seller with 10,000 orders apply to you on order #1. TikTok does place new shops into a Shop Probation period during onboarding, but AHR scoring and metric enforcement are active from your first order. Probation does not reduce or delay compliance consequences.

Most onboarding guides stop at "upload your ID and link your bank account." This one starts where they stop: the compliance setup that determines whether your shop survives the first 30 days.

## Why Most New Seller Guides Are Incomplete

Every TikTok Shop onboarding tutorial covers the same five steps: register, verify identity, add products, set up shipping, link bank account.

**None of them mention that your Account Health Rating (AHR) is live from day one.**

A low-volume shop can exceed the 4% LDR threshold with a single late order. A Friday evening order is not late just because it sits over the weekend (weekends are not business days), but it becomes late if it is not In Transit within 2 business days of entering Awaiting Shipment status. With only 10 total orders, one missed deadline puts you at 10% LDR.

### What AHR score do new TikTok Shop sellers start with?

Every new TikTok Shop seller starts with 200 Account Health Rating (AHR) points on a 0-1,000 scale where higher means healthier. According to TikTok's Account Health Rating documentation (updated March 2026), enforcement milestones occur at 150, 100, 50, and 0 points:
- At 150 points, sellers lose the ability to create new listings for 7 days
- At 100 points, restrictions extend to 14 days
- At 50 points, 28 days
- At 0, the account is permanently deactivated

New sellers earn 4 AHR points per 200 completed orders, up to 20 points per week. At low order volume (50 orders/month), that means roughly 1 point of recovery per month. As of March 2026, AHR scoring is active from the first order, and TikTok's Shop Probation during onboarding does not delay metric enforcement. Sellers should treat those initial 200 points as a non-renewable resource during the first 30 days.

## The 2026 New Seller Onboarding Checklist

### Step 1: Complete Registration (But Don't Stop Here)

**The basics still apply:**

- **Individual sellers:** Valid government ID, proof of address, linked bank account
- **Business sellers:** Business license, EIN/tax documents, company bank details
- **Tax info:** Submit W-9 with valid TIN to avoid 24% withholding on payouts
- **Official TikTok account:** Link one account per shop (becomes a Business Account)

Approval takes 3-5 business days. Use this time for Steps 2-7.

### Step 2: Configure Your Warehouse and Pickup Schedule

This is where compliance starts. Your warehouse setup directly affects whether orders ship on time.

- Set your pickup address (up to 20 allowed via Multi-Warehouse)
- Set one return address
- Align carrier pickup time with order processing window
- Set realistic warehouse hours (don't pretend you can process Saturday if you can't)

**The math that kills new sellers:** A Friday 6 PM order has a dispatch deadline of Tuesday 11:59 PM PST (2 business days, skipping Saturday and Sunday). If your carrier doesn't pick up until Wednesday morning, that order is late.

### Step 3: Understand Business Day Counting

TikTok's dispatch SLA is 2 business days. This sounds simple. It isn't.

TikTok Shop's dispatch SLA is 2 business days, and the counting rules are specific. According to TikTok's dispatch policy documentation (updated March 2026), business days are Monday through Friday only, excluding weekends and US federal holidays. The order placement day does not count toward the SLA.

| Holiday | Date | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Memorial Day | Mon, May 25 | Thursday orders due Tuesday (Monday skipped) |
| Independence Day | Fri, Jul 3 (observed) | Wednesday orders due Monday Jul 6 |
| Labor Day | Mon, Sep 7 | Thursday orders due Tuesday (Monday skipped) |
| Thanksgiving | Thu, Nov 26 | Tuesday orders due Friday Nov 27 |
| Christmas | Fri, Dec 25 | Wednesday orders due Monday Dec 28 |

### Step 4: Set Up Your Carrier Correctly

As of January 26, 2026, TikTok requires that orders are **picked up and scanned by carriers** within the 2-business-day SLA. The order status must change to **In Transit** in Seller Center.

**Label printing does not count as shipped.** Printing a label does not count. Marking "shipped" in ShipStation or Shopify does not count if TikTok's system still shows "Awaiting Shipment." The carrier scan is the only thing that matters.

- Confirmed pickup time with carrier (what time do they scan?)
- Verified scan appears in TikTok Seller Center within 24 hours
- Tested with 1-2 orders before scaling
- Backup drop-off location identified for emergencies

### Step 5: Learn Your Four Compliance Metrics

TikTok monitors four metrics that affect your AHR. Violate any of them, and you lose points.

| Metric | Threshold | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Late Dispatch Rate (LDR)** | ≤ 4%; >10% enforcement | % of orders not In Transit within 2 business days |
| **Valid Tracking Rate (VTR)** | ≥ 95% | % of orders with accurate, verifiable tracking numbers |
| **Seller Fault Cancellation Rate (SFCR)** | ≤ 2.5% | % of orders cancelled due to seller error |
| **On-Time Delivery Rate (OTDR)** | ≥ 80% | % of orders delivered within 6 business days |

**Why LDR is your biggest risk as a new seller:** With low order volume, every single late order has outsized impact. If you have 20 orders in your first week and 1 ships late, that's a 5% LDR, already above the 4% recommendation. Two late orders = 10%, which is at the enforcement boundary.

### Step 6: Build Your First-Week Dispatch Workflow

Your first week is the highest-risk period. You're learning the platform, your carrier relationship is untested, and your order volume is unpredictable.

- **10 AM:** Check Seller Center for orders in "Awaiting Shipment" status
- **2 PM:** Process and pack all orders. Leave buffer before carrier pickup.
- **4 PM:** Carrier handoff. Confirm physical pickup, not just label creation.
- **8 PM:** Verify scan. Check that TikTok Seller Center shows "In Transit."
- **10 PM:** End-of-day audit. Any order still in "Awaiting Shipment" with a deadline tomorrow is a T-12 alert situation.

**Weekend protocol:** Friday and Saturday orders don't start counting until Monday. Process weekend orders on Monday morning, first thing. Deadline for most weekend orders: Tuesday 11:59 PM PST.

### Step 7: Set Up Compliance Monitoring From Day One

Your first 30 days determine your trajectory. One bad week can drop your AHR below 150 (listing restrictions for 7 days) before you've even built momentum.

- Check AHR score in Seller Center daily
- Calculate your LDR manually: (late orders / total orders) x 100
- Track every carrier scan time (is it same-day or next-day?)
- Document any delays with timestamps (for appeals if needed)
- Review dispatch SLA deadlines every morning

## The First 30 Days: What Separates Surviving Sellers From Suspended Ones

### Sellers who survive the first 30 days

- Ship zero late orders in week one (even if that means only listing 5 products initially)

**Result:** AHR stable at 200+

### Sellers who get AHR restrictions in the first 30 days

- List 50+ products before confirming their dispatch workflow
- Assume weekend orders can wait until Monday afternoon
- Use a carrier that scans packages 24-48 hours after pickup
- Don't check Seller Center daily

**Result:** AHR below 150, listing restrictions

The difference isn't luck. It's preparation.

## Common New Seller Onboarding Mistakes

**Mistake #1: Listing products before your dispatch workflow is tested**
List 3-5 products. Ship a few orders. Confirm carrier scan times. Then scale. Your AHR doesn't care about your product catalog ambitions.

**Mistake #2: Ignoring the 48-hour deadline article because "I'll ship fast"**
It's 2 business days, not 48 clock hours. The distinction matters most around weekends and holidays.

**Mistake #3: Assuming your shipping integration handles everything**
ShipStation, Shopify, and other platforms can show "shipped" status while TikTok Seller Center still shows "Awaiting Shipment." The carrier scan is what TikTok checks. Verify integration sync by testing with real orders.

**Mistake #4: Not submitting your W-9 immediately**
Without a valid TIN on file, TikTok withholds 24% of your payouts. This isn't a compliance metric, but it's cash flow that new sellers can't afford to lose.

**Mistake #5: Skipping the new seller promotion**
Sellers who achieve their first sale within 60 days of completing onboarding receive a discounted 3% referral fee rate for 30 days. Don't leave money on the table.

## FAQ

### Does TikTok give new sellers a grace period for compliance metrics?
Not for AHR scoring. As of March 2026, your Account Health Rating starts at 200 points on day one, and the same LDR, VTR, SFCR, and OTDR thresholds apply to your first order as they do to your ten-thousandth.

### What's the fastest way to tank my AHR as a new seller?
Missing dispatch deadlines on low volume. If you have 15 orders and 2 ship late, your LDR is 13.3%. That's above the 10% enforcement trigger.

### How long does TikTok Shop seller approval take?
Registration approval takes 3-5 business days. Use the waiting period to set up your warehouse, test your carrier, understand business day counting, and configure your dispatch workflow.

### What happens if my AHR drops below 150 in my first month?
You lose the ability to create new listings and enroll in promotional campaigns for 7 days. For a new seller still building their catalog, this is devastating. At 100 points, restrictions extend to 14 days. At 50, it's 28 days. At 0, permanent deactivation.
